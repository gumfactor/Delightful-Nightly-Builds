# Personal Profile

I approach AI primarily as a force multiplier for human expertise rather than a replacement for it. Most of my projects sit at the intersection of human judgment and intelligent automation: helping researchers, students, consumers, or knowledge workers make better decisions while preserving the strengths that people bring to the process. I tend to think in systems, workflows, and long time horizons, and I am usually more interested in building durable infrastructure than one-off experiments.

Whether I am running a neuroscience lab, building consumer technology, developing AI workflows, or exploring quantitative investing, my goal is generally the same: reduce friction, preserve context, automate repetitive work, and free up more time for higher-level thinking and creativity.
